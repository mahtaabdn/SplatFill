Abstract

3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) has enabled realistic 3D scene representations from multi-view images. However, inpainting missing regions, whether due to occlusion or scene editing, remains a challenging task, often leading to blurry details, artifacts, and inconsistent geometry. We introduce SplatFill, a novel approach for 3DGS scene inpainting that achieves state-of-the-art perceptual quality with 24.5% faster training than existing methods. Our method combines scale-invariant depth supervision using soft depth clustering and crop-focused refinement, a consistency-aware Selective Guided Inpainting (SGI) scheme that iteratively identifies and corrects the most inconsistent regions, and object-aware supervision for better semantic boundary preservation. Evaluations on the SPIn-NeRF dataset show that SplatFill surpasses prior NeRF-based and 3DGS-based inpainting methods in visual fidelity while delivering sharper high-frequency details and stronger geometric coherence across viewpoints.

Method

Scale-Invariant Depth Supervision

Soft Depth Clustering Loss and Crop-Focused Depth Loss guide Gaussian placement without explicit scale-shift alignment between monocular and rendered depth.

Selective Guided Inpainting (SGI)

Instead of re-inpainting every view, SGI localizes the most inconsistent regions and refines only those regions, improving consistency while keeping the pipeline efficient.

Object-Aware Supervision

Instance-level features are embedded into Gaussians to better preserve semantic boundaries and support object-aware scene editing.

Highlights

  • Targets the trade-off between single-view detail and multi-view consistency in 3DGS inpainting.
  • Uses depth-guided optimization to improve 3D Gaussian placement and scene geometry.
  • Applies view-specific refinement only where depth inconsistency is highest.
  • Achieves stronger perceptual quality on SPIn-NeRF with improved efficiency.
